Kid's Dictionary

Apple

A round fruit with red, green, or yellow skin that is white inside and has seeds.

Banana

A long curved yellow fruit that grows in bunches.

Cat

A small furry animal that has four legs and makes a meowing sound.

Dog

A furry animal with four legs that barks and is kept as a pet.

Elephant

A very large animal with big ears, a long trunk, and tusks. Elephants live in Africa and Asia.

Frog

A small animal that lives near water, has smooth skin, long back legs for jumping, and makes a croaking sound.

Giraffe

A very tall animal with a long neck, spots, and small horns on its head. Giraffes live in Africa.

Horse

A large animal with four legs that people can ride. Horses usually have a mane and tail.

Igloo

A dome-shaped shelter made from blocks of snow, traditionally built by Inuit people.

Jellyfish

A sea creature with a soft, jelly-like body and long, stinging tentacles.

Kangaroo

An animal from Australia that carries its young in a pouch and can hop and jump using its long back legs.

Lion

A large wild cat with fur around its face like a mane. Lions hunt and live in groups called prides.

Monkey

A furry animal with a long tail that lives in trees and can swing from branch to branch.

Nest

A structure where birds lay eggs and raise baby birds. Nests are made from materials like grass, twigs, and feathers.

Octopus

A sea creature with eight long arms and the ability to change color and texture to blend in with its surroundings.

Penguin

A black and white flightless bird that lives in cold climates and swims well using its flippers.

Quail

A small, round bird with a short tail and mottled brown plumage.

Rabbit

A small furry animal with long ears, short fluffy tail and strong back legs for hopping.

Snake

A long, thin reptile without legs. Snakes slither along the ground.

Tiger

A large wild cat with orange fur and black stripes. Tigers are powerful hunters and live mainly in Asia.

Umbrella

A device with folding metal arms and a waterproof fabric canopy that protects a person from rain.

Vulture

A large bird of prey with featherless heads that feeds mainly on carrion.

Whale

A very large sea mammal that breathes air through a blowhole on top of its head.

X-Ray Fish

A fish that can see using x-ray vision to find prey and avoid predators in murky waters.

Yak

A long-haired bovine mammal adapted to living in high, cold mountain regions of Central Asia.

Zebra

An African equine mammal with black and white stripes and an erect mane.

Scar

A mark left after injured tissue has healed.
